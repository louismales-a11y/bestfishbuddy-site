Fishing in Western Manitoba: Parklands & Duck Mountain

Western Manitoba offers a mix of prairie rivers, boreal forest lakes, and the high-country trout fishing of Duck Mountain and Riding Mountain — a hidden gem for anglers willing to explore.

Western Manitoba is often overlooked by anglers who head east to the Whiteshell or north to Lake Winnipeg. But the Parklands region — Duck Mountain, Riding Mountain, and the Souris River — offers exceptional fishing with far fewer crowds. The region's clear lakes, cold trout streams, and prairie rivers provide diverse fishing opportunities for trout, walleye, pike, and smallmouth bass.

1. Duck Mountain Provincial Park (Rainbow Trout, Brook Trout & Brown Trout)

3.5 hours northwest of Winnipeg

Duck Mountain Provincial Park is the premier trout fishing destination in Manitoba. The park's clear, cold lakes are stocked with rainbow trout, brook trout, and brown trout. East Blue Lake, West Blue Lake, and Wellman Lake are the most popular destinations, with rainbow trout averaging 1-3 pounds and larger fish available. The park's streams also hold brook trout. Duck Mountain has excellent campgrounds, hiking trails, and a resort at Wellman Lake. The park is at a higher elevation than surrounding areas, offering cooler temperatures and stunning fall colours. Fly fishing and light spinning tackle are the most productive approaches.

2. Riding Mountain National Park (Rainbow Trout, Brook Trout & Northern Pike)

3 hours northwest of Winnipeg

Riding Mountain National Park offers excellent fishing in a stunning natural setting. Clear Lake, the park's most popular lake, holds rainbow trout, brook trout, and northern pike. The lake is deep, clear, and supports a healthy fish population. The park's backcountry lakes and streams offer more remote fishing opportunities. A national park permit is required in addition to a Manitoba fishing licence. The town of Wasagaming offers accommodations, restaurants, and outfitters. The park is also known for its bison herd and excellent hiking trails.

3. Souris River (Smallmouth Bass, Walleye & Northern Pike)

3 hours southwest of Winnipeg

The Souris River flows through southwestern Manitoba and offers excellent fishing for smallmouth bass, walleye, and northern pike. The river's current, deep pools, and rocky structure provide diverse habitat. Smallmouth bass fishing is particularly good, with fish in the 1-3 pound range common. The river is accessible from several points near the town of Souris and through the Souris River Bend Wildlife Management Area. The river is also popular for canoeing and kayaking.

4. Lake Metigoshe & Turtle Mountain (Walleye, Northern Pike & Perch)

3.5 hours southwest of Winnipeg

Lake Metigoshe, in Turtle Mountain Provincial Park, offers excellent fishing for walleye, northern pike, and yellow perch. The lake straddles the Canada-US border, with the southern portion in North Dakota. The Canadian side offers good access and several campgrounds. The lake's shallow, productive waters hold healthy fish populations. Turtle Mountain Provincial Park has excellent hiking and biking trails in addition to fishing.

5. Assiniboine River (Westman) (Walleye, Channel Catfish & Goldeye)

Through Brandon area, 2 hours west of Winnipeg

The Assiniboine River flows through Brandon and offers good fishing for walleye, channel catfish, and goldeye. The river through Brandon has several access points in parks and along the river trail. Walleye fishing is best in spring and fall. Channel catfish provide excellent sport in the summer months. The river is also popular for paddling.

6. Oak Lake (Walleye, Northern Pike & Perch)

2.5 hours west of Winnipeg

Oak Lake, located near Virden, offers excellent fishing for walleye, northern pike, and yellow perch. The lake is one of the larger lakes in southwestern Manitoba and holds healthy fish populations. Oak Lake has a popular beach and campground, making it a great family destination. The lake's stained water provides good fishing during daylight hours.

7. Rock Lake (Walleye, Northern Pike & Perch)

3 hours west of Winnipeg

Rock Lake, near the community of Killarney, offers good fishing for walleye, northern pike, and yellow perch. The lake is part of the Pembina River system and has good access and amenities. Rock Lake is a popular summer destination with a beach, campground, and nearby golf course.

8. Pembina River (Brook Trout & Brown Trout)

2 hours southwest of Winnipeg

The Pembina River, flowing through the Pembina Valley, offers trout fishing in a scenic valley setting. The river's cold, spring-fed waters hold brook trout and brown trout. The Pembina Valley is a beautiful region with deep river valleys and unique ecosystems. The river is accessible from several points along Highway 3. The Pembina Valley Provincial Park offers camping and hiking.

9. Whitewater Lake (Northern Pike, Walleye & Perch)

3 hours southwest of Winnipeg

Whitewater Lake, near Boissevain, offers good fishing for northern pike, walleye, and yellow perch. The lake's shallow, productive waters hold healthy fish populations. Whitewater Lake is also an important bird area, making it a great destination for anglers who also enjoy birdwatching.

10. Shell River &Area (Brook Trout, Rainbow Trout)

3.5 hours northwest of Winnipeg

The Shell River and its tributaries, near the community of Roblin, offer excellent trout fishing. The cold, spring-fed streams hold brook trout and rainbow trout. The area is less developed than Duck Mountain, offering a more remote experience. The Shell River is a good option for anglers looking to explore new water.

Fishing Tips for Western Manitoba

  • Focus on trout in Duck Mountain — Duck Mountain is Manitoba's premier trout destination. The park's lakes are stocked regularly and produce excellent fish. Spring and fall are the best seasons.
  • Try the Souris River for bass — The Souris River offers the best smallmouth bass fishing in western Manitoba. Focus on rocky sections and current breaks.
  • Book park accommodations early — Riding Mountain and Duck Mountain are popular summer destinations. Campgrounds and resort rooms book well in advance.
  • Explore Turtle Mountain — Lake Metigoshe and Turtle Mountain Provincial Park offer excellent fishing in a unique cross-border setting.
  • Watch for ticks — The Parklands region has significant tick populations in spring and early summer. Check yourself after fishing, especially in grassy or brushy areas.
