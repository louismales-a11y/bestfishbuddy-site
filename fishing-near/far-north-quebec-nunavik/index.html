Fishing in Far North Quebec & Nunavik

Arctic char, trophy lake trout, and brook trout in Quebec's last wilderness. Caniapiscau, Mistassini, and the Ungava rivers.

1. Caniapiscau Reservoir

Far northern Quebec

The Caniapiscau Reservoir is the largest reservoir in Quebec, offering trophy lake trout and northern pike fishing.

2. Lake Mistassini

Northern Quebec

Lake Mistassini is the largest natural lake in Quebec, offering excellent lake trout, walleye, and northern pike fishing.

3. Lake Albanel

Mistassini region

Lake Albanel offers excellent lake trout and northern pike fishing near Lake Mistassini.

4. Lake Bienville

Far northern Quebec

Lake Bienville offers trophy lake trout and Arctic char fishing in a remote wilderness setting.

5. Lake Minto

Nunavik

Lake Minto offers Arctic char and lake trout fishing in the Ungava region.

6. Koksoak River

Nunavik

The Koksoak River offers Arctic char and Atlantic salmon fishing flowing into Ungava Bay.

7. George River

Nunavik

The George River offers excellent Arctic char and brook trout fishing in a remote tundra setting.

8. Leaf River

Nunavik

The Leaf River offers Arctic char and brook trout fishing in northern Nunavik.

9. Whale River

Nunavik

The Whale River offers Arctic char and brook trout fishing in the Ungava region.

10. Koroc River

Nunavik

The Koroc River offers Arctic char fishing in a stunning fjord setting in northern Nunavik.

11. Payne River

Nunavik

The Payne River offers Arctic char and brook trout fishing in the Ungava Bay region.

12. Lake Payne

Nunavik

Lake Payne offers Arctic char and lake trout fishing in the Ungava region.

13. Lake Sakami

James Bay region

Lake Sakami offers northern pike and walleye fishing in the James Bay region.

14. Opinaca Reservoir

James Bay region

The Opinaca Reservoir offers northern pike and walleye fishing in the James Bay hydroelectric region.

15. Eastmain Reservoir

James Bay

The Eastmain Reservoir offers northern pike and walleye fishing in the James Bay region.

16. Robert-Bourassa Reservoir

James Bay

The Robert-Bourassa Reservoir offers northern pike and walleye fishing in the James Bay hydroelectric complex.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at peche.mffp.gouv.qc.ca. Nunavik requires a separate supplementary licence.
  • Plan extensively -- Nunavik fly-in fishing trips book a year or more in advance. Plan well ahead.
  • Bring warm clothing -- Even in July, Nunavik can experience near-freezing temperatures and strong winds.
  • Respect the resource -- These northern fisheries are fragile. Practise careful catch-and-release.
  • Book a fly-in trip -- The best way to access Nunavik's remote fishing is through fly-in lodges and outfitters.
  • Get a licence — All anglers 18+ need an Ontario fishing licence. Buy online at ontario.ca/fishing.
  • Watch Great Lakes weather — Lake Erie is notorious for sudden, dangerous storms. Check marine forecasts before heading out.
  • Hire a charter — For Lake Ontario salmon and Lake Erie walleye, chartered trips are the most productive option.
  • Fish the Bay of Quinte in fall — September through November offers the best trophy walleye fishing in the bay.
  • Try the Niagara River — The Niagara River offers year-round fishing opportunities unmatched in Ontario.
