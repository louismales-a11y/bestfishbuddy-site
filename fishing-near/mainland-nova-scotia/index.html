Fishing in Mainland Nova Scotia

Atlantic salmon, brook trout, smallmouth bass, striped bass -- from the South Shore to the Bay of Fundy.

1. Margaree River (Mainland)

Inverness County

The Margaree River is one of Nova Scotia's most famous Atlantic salmon rivers, offering world-class salmon and sea-run brook trout fishing.

2. Medway River

Queens County

The Medway River offers excellent Atlantic salmon and brown trout fishing in southwestern Nova Scotia.

3. LaHave River

Lunenburg County

The LaHave River offers Atlantic salmon, rainbow trout, and brown trout fishing in a scenic setting.

4. St. Mary's River

Guysborough County

The St. Mary's River is a premier Atlantic salmon river on Nova Scotia's Eastern Shore.

5. Liscomb River

Guysborough area

The Liscomb River offers Atlantic salmon and brook trout fishing on the Eastern Shore.

6. Sheet Harbour River

Sheet Harbour area

The Sheet Harbour River offers Atlantic salmon and brook trout fishing on the Eastern Shore.

7. Tangier River

Tangier area

The Tangier River offers Atlantic salmon and brook trout fishing.

8. Musquodoboit River

Musquodoboit area

The Musquodoboit River offers Atlantic salmon and brook trout fishing in the Halifax region.

9. Sackville River

Bedford/Sackville

The Sackville River offers brook trout fishing in the Halifax area.

10. Shubenacadie River

Shubenacadie area

The Shubenacadie River offers excellent striped bass fishing. The famous tidal bore creates unique fishing conditions.

11. Shubenacadie Grand Lake

Shubenacadie area

Shubenacadie Grand Lake offers smallmouth bass and white perch fishing.

12. Lake Rossignol

Liverpool area

Lake Rossignol is the premier smallmouth bass lake in Nova Scotia, offering excellent fishing in a large reservoir.

13. Kejimkujik Lake

Kejimkujik Park

Kejimkujik Lake offers brook trout and white perch fishing in Kejimkujik National Park.

14. Peskowesk Lake

Kejimkujik area

Peskowesk Lake offers brook trout fishing in the Kejimkujik backcountry.

15. Big Dam Lake

Kejimkujik area

Big Dam Lake offers brook trout fishing in the Kejimkujik area.

16. Beech Hill Lake

Kejimkujik area

Beech Hill Lake offers brook trout fishing near Kejimkujik National Park.

17. Lake Milo

Yarmouth area

Lake Milo offers brook trout and smallmouth bass fishing near Yarmouth.

18. Vaughan Lake

Yarmouth area

Vaughan Lake offers brook trout fishing in southwestern Nova Scotia.

19. Ogden Lake

Yarmouth area

Ogden Lake offers brook trout fishing in the Yarmouth area.

20. Lake Fanning

Digby area

Lake Fanning offers brook trout fishing near Digby.

21. Brierly Brook

Antigonish area

Brierly Brook offers brook trout and Atlantic salmon fishing in the Antigonish region.

22. Pomquet River

Antigonish area

The Pomquet River offers Atlantic salmon and brook trout fishing in Antigonish County.

23. Sunnybrae River

Pictou area

The Sunnybrae River offers brook trout and Atlantic salmon fishing.

24. East River (Pictou)

Pictou area

The East River offers Atlantic salmon and brook trout fishing near Pictou.

25. Annapolis River

Annapolis Valley

The Annapolis River offers brook trout and smallmouth bass fishing in the Annapolis Valley.

26. Gaspereau River

Wolfville area

The Gaspereau River offers brook trout and brown trout fishing in the Annapolis Valley.

27. Avon River

Windsor area

The Avon River offers striped bass and brook trout fishing.

28. Mersey River

Liverpool area

The Mersey River offers brook trout and brown trout fishing flowing into Lake Rossignol.

29. Tusket River

Tusket area

The Tusket River offers Atlantic salmon and brook trout fishing in Yarmouth County.

30. Sissiboo River

Weymouth area

The Sissiboo River offers Atlantic salmon and brook trout fishing in Digby County.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- All anglers 16+ need a Nova Scotia fishing licence. Buy online at novascotia.ca/fishing.
  • Try the Shubenacadie -- The Shubenacadie River offers world-class striped bass fishing. Spring and fall are best.
  • Fish smallmouth bass -- Lake Rossignol offers the best smallmouth bass fishing in Nova Scotia.
  • Watch the tides -- Nova Scotia has the highest tides in the world. Many rivers are tidal -- check tide tables.
  • Respect the salmon -- Atlantic salmon populations are fragile. Practise careful catch-and-release.
  • Get a licence — All anglers 18+ need an Ontario fishing licence. Buy online at ontario.ca/fishing.
  • Watch Great Lakes weather — Lake Erie is notorious for sudden, dangerous storms. Check marine forecasts before heading out.
  • Hire a charter — For Lake Ontario salmon and Lake Erie walleye, chartered trips are the most productive option.
  • Fish the Bay of Quinte in fall — September through November offers the best trophy walleye fishing in the bay.
  • Try the Niagara River — The Niagara River offers year-round fishing opportunities unmatched in Ontario.
