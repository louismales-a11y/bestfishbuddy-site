Fishing in the Laurentians & Lanaudiere

Brook trout, lake trout, walleye, and pike in Quebec's classic cottage country north of Montreal.

1. Lake des Sables

Sainte-Agathe-des-Monts

Lake des Sables offers smallmouth bass, walleye, and northern pike fishing in the heart of the Laurentians.

2. Lake Archambault

Sainte-Anne-des-Plaines

Lake Archambault offers brook trout and rainbow trout fishing in Lanaudiere.

3. Lake Maskinonge

Saint-Gabriel area

Lake Maskinonge offers northern pike, walleye, and perch fishing in Lanaudiere.

4. Lake Simon

Saint-Michel-des-Saints

Lake Simon offers lake trout, brook trout, and walleye fishing in Lanaudiere.

5. Lake Taureau

Saint-Michel-des-Saints

Lake Taureau offers walleye, northern pike, and smallmouth bass fishing in a large reservoir.

6. Lake Sacacomie

7. Lake à la Tortue

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at peche.mffp.gouv.qc.ca.
  • Fish the Laurentians -- The Laurentians offer excellent brook trout and lake trout fishing in a classic Quebec cottage setting.
  • Try Lanaudiere -- Lanaudiere has hundreds of lakes offering quality fishing with easy access from Montreal.
  • Book accommodation early -- The Laurentians are a popular summer destination. Book well in advance.
  • Bring insect repellent -- The Laurentians have significant mosquito populations in early summer.
  • Get a licence — All anglers 18+ need an Ontario fishing licence. Buy online at ontario.ca/fishing.
  • Watch Great Lakes weather — Lake Erie is notorious for sudden, dangerous storms. Check marine forecasts before heading out.
  • Hire a charter — For Lake Ontario salmon and Lake Erie walleye, chartered trips are the most productive option.
  • Fish the Bay of Quinte in fall — September through November offers the best trophy walleye fishing in the bay.
  • Try the Niagara River — The Niagara River offers year-round fishing opportunities unmatched in Ontario.
