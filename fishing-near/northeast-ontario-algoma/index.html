Fishing in Northeast Ontario & Algoma

Sudbury, Algoma, Chapleau, Wawa -- brook trout, walleye, pike, and lake trout in Ontario's boreal shield.

1. Chapleau Crown Game Preserve

Chapleau area

The Chapleau Crown Game Preserve offers remote brook trout fishing in pristine shield lakes that are fly-in or hike-in only.

2. Wawa Area Lakes

Wawa

The Wawa area offers excellent brook trout, lake trout, and northern pike fishing in hundreds of shield lakes.

3. Lake Superior Provincial Park

Wawa to Sault Ste. Marie

Lake Superior Provincial Park offers excellent brook trout and lake trout fishing in its inland lakes and rivers.

4. Batchawana River

North of Sault Ste. Marie

The Batchawana River offers brook trout, walleye, and northern pike fishing flowing into Lake Superior.

5. Spanish River

Webbwood area

The Spanish River offers excellent walleye, northern pike, and smallmouth bass fishing flowing into Lake Huron.

6. Mississagi River

Elliot Lake area

The Mississagi River offers walleye, northern pike, and brook trout fishing. A designated Canadian Heritage River.

7. Lake Nipissing

North Bay area

Lake Nipissing offers excellent walleye and northern pike fishing in its shallow, productive waters.

8. Temagami Region

Temagami

The Temagami region offers excellent lake trout, brook trout, and walleye fishing in pristine shield lakes.

9. Algonquin Park

Huntsville to Barry's Bay

Algonquin Provincial Park offers excellent brook trout, lake trout, and smallmouth bass in hundreds of remote lakes.

10. Killarney Provincial Park

Killarney

Killarney Park offers excellent lake trout and smallmouth bass fishing in its clear, scenic lakes.

11. Sudbury Area Lakes

Sudbury

The Sudbury area has numerous lakes offering walleye, pike, and bass fishing.

12. Manitoulin Island

Manitoulin Island

Manitoulin Island has numerous inland lakes offering excellent fishing for bass, pike, and walleye.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at ontario.ca/fishing.
  • Try brook trout -- Algoma offers some of the best brook trout fishing in Ontario.
  • Explore the Chapleau Preserve -- The Chapleau Crown Game Preserve offers remote, untouched fishing.
  • Bring insect repellent -- Algoma has intense mosquitoes and black flies.
  • Book canoe trips -- The Spanish and Mississagi rivers offer excellent multi-day canoe fishing.
  • Get a licence — All anglers 18+ need an Ontario fishing licence. Buy online at ontario.ca/fishing.
  • Watch Great Lakes weather — Lake Erie is notorious for sudden, dangerous storms. Check marine forecasts before heading out.
  • Hire a charter — For Lake Ontario salmon and Lake Erie walleye, chartered trips are the most productive option.
  • Fish the Bay of Quinte in fall — September through November offers the best trophy walleye fishing in the bay.
  • Try the Niagara River — The Niagara River offers year-round fishing opportunities unmatched in Ontario.
