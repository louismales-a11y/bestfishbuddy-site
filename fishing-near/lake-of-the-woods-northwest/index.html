Fishing in Lake of the Woods & Northwest Ontario

Kenora, Lake of the Woods, Rainy Lake, Sioux Narrows -- world-class walleye, smallmouth bass, muskie, and pike.

1. Lake of the Woods

Kenora area

Lake of the Woods is one of North America's premier fishing destinations with over 14,000 islands providing endless fishing for walleye, bass, pike, and muskie.

2. Rainy Lake

Fort Frances area

Rainy Lake offers excellent walleye, smallmouth bass, and northern pike fishing. The lake straddles the Ontario-Minnesota border.

3. Sioux Narrows

Sioux Narrows area

Sioux Narrows on Lake of the Woods offers exceptional fishing with numerous lodges and outfitters.

4. Eagle Lake

Dryden area

Eagle Lake is known for excellent walleye and smallmouth bass fishing in clear waters.

5. Lac Seul

Ear Falls area

Lac Seul is widely considered one of the best walleye lakes in Ontario. The flooded timber holds trophy fish.

6. Wabigoon Lake

Dryden area

Wabigoon Lake offers excellent walleye and northern pike fishing near the Trans-Canada Highway.

7. Dinorwic Lake

Dryden area

Dinorwic Lake offers excellent walleye and muskie fishing with less pressure than Lac Seul.

8. Separation Lake

Vermilion Bay area

Separation Lake offers excellent walleye and smallmouth bass fishing connecting to Lake of the Woods.

9. Clay Lake

Near Vermilion Bay

Clay Lake offers excellent walleye and northern pike fishing with healthy fish populations.

Manitou Lake offers excellent walleye and northern pike fishing in a remote setting.

Fishing Tips

  • Get a licence -- Buy online at ontario.ca/fishing.
  • Book lodges early -- Lake of the Woods lodges book months in advance.
  • Fish walleye in spring -- May and June offer the best walleye fishing in the Kenora area.
  • Try muskie in fall -- September and October offer excellent muskie fishing.
  • Bring insect repellent -- Northwestern Ontario has significant mosquito populations in early summer.
  • Get a licence — All anglers 18+ need an Ontario fishing licence. Buy online at ontario.ca/fishing.
  • Watch Great Lakes weather — Lake Erie is notorious for sudden, dangerous storms. Check marine forecasts before heading out.
  • Hire a charter — For Lake Ontario salmon and Lake Erie walleye, chartered trips are the most productive option.
  • Fish the Bay of Quinte in fall — September through November offers the best trophy walleye fishing in the bay.
  • Try the Niagara River — The Niagara River offers year-round fishing opportunities unmatched in Ontario.
